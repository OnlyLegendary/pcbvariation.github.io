Projects I actually built

This is my personal engineering portfolio. I mess with PCB design, embedded firmware, and real prototypes you can hold. I like showing what worked, what didn’t, and why I made the choices I did.

Battery Logger Board

I needed a tiny board that could read cell voltage, temperature, and log data on the go. I built the PCB in KiCad, routed the power stuff by hand, and wrote the firmware to sleep most of the time so the battery lasts.

USB-C Sensor Hub

A small board that fans out USB-C power to a bunch of sensors and reads them over I2C. I had to make sure the layout didn’t get noisy, so I split the ground planes and kept the traces short.

  • USB-C
  • Signal Integrity
  • Firmware

Desk Test Fixture

I got tired of plugging in probes, so I made a pogo-pin test fixture for quick checks. It has a simple microcontroller that shows pass/fail and dumps serial logs to my laptop.

  • Prototyping
  • Fixture
  • Serial

Motor Driver Rev B

My first motor driver ran hot, so I rebuilt it with thicker traces, a better gate driver, and some airflow cutouts in the enclosure. It runs cooler and doesn’t freak out at higher current.
