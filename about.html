About me

I’m a teen who keeps building hardware because it’s the fastest way to learn. I design PCBs, write firmware, and make prototypes that actually run on my desk. If something fails, I poke at it until I figure out why.

How I work

I start with the problem, sketch the circuit, then jump into KiCad. I keep my layouts simple and label everything so future-me doesn’t get lost. Once the board shows up, I solder it, flash the code, and test it with a multimeter and a cheap scope.

Why this site exists

I wanted a place to document what I’m building, not just post pretty photos. I’ll share the weird issues, the quick fixes, and what I learned so other people can steal the good ideas.

What I’m into

Power electronics, sensors, tiny boards that do big stuff, and clean firmware that doesn’t waste cycles. If it has wires and a microcontroller, I’m probably interested.

My build loop

  1. Sketch it. Figure out the job, then draw the circuit and block diagram.
  2. Route it. Keep traces short, power clean, and labels obvious.
  3. Solder it. Build it fast, then stare at it until it works.
  4. Break it. Push it on purpose so I know the limits.
  5. Fix it. Rev the board, clean the firmware, repeat.
